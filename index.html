The Town

A living, breathing hollow

Hand-placed, not generated: a fountain plaza, a creaking inn, a smithy with a working forge, market stalls, cottages, a barn — and the old cemetery on the hill, fenced as if the fence were for keeping something in. Villagers wander, lanterns burn, and dusk never quite lifts.

The town of Raven Hollow at golden dusk — every building placed by hand, every villager on their own path.

Nineteen townsfolk with their own words — and their own worries.

The plaza well, the lantern light, the road south.

Combat

Spells with weight

Mouse-aimed, cooldown-driven combat against the things that walk out of the fog — skeletons by the graveyard, orc camps past the farm, and worse to come.

Paladin — Consecration burns in a golden ring.

Necromancer — Raise Dead answers to you, for a while.

Mage — Fireball, delivered.

And beneath it all…

Set in Draconia — an original dark-fantasy universe of four monstrous factions locked in a cold war, a buried world-machine, and a language that should never be read aloud. The town is warm. The world is not.

Loot

Legendaries with stories

A WoW-style backpack and paper-doll: drag, equip, and watch the gear appear on your character — in the sheet and in the world. Rarity tiers from common grey to legendary orange, each legendary with its own lore and living effect.

Emberfall and the Bulwark equipped — “The blade that lit the hollow's last lantern.”

The Systems

A whole RPG under the hood

Not a vertical slice with the depth painted on — the real machinery of a classic MMO-scale RPG is here and playable: a talent forest per class, faction reputations, a mount stable, an auction house, a PvP ladder, runewords, a living calendar, legendary questlines. Press ~ in game to open the menu and reach all of it.

The atlas of Draconia — four kingdoms and the drowned Collector's Coast, hand-lettered on gothic parchment, WoW-style zoom from world to your own footsteps.

Three talent trees per class — Pit-Fighter, Shieldwall, Warcaller — 31-point classic builds.

Seven legendaries, each a questline and a living effect. What you carry is what they see — no transmog, ever.

Six factions of Draconia, from Hated to Exalted — the Strigoi, the Varcolaci, the Iele, and more.

Seven professions, skill to 1000, recipes that drop in the world — right up to craftable legendaries.

The Reckoning Floor — a 14-rank Accord Roll, 1v1 arena, titles you fight under.

Thirty-one mounts — trainer stock, elite trophies, faction steeds, wild tames from the Bone-Steed to the Great Elk.

A deed-book of achievements across nine categories, with titles and mounts as rewards.

One menu, every system a keystroke away — the whole game opens from here.
